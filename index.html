THE MANIFESTO

"A Written Testimony: MAKTUB is a sonic pilgrimage across the Atlantic — a dialogue between the descendants of the transatlantic divide, bound by shared struggle, divine purpose, and ancestral memory. Inspired by Minister Louis Farrakhan’s visits to South Africa in 1996 and 1998, the album imagines the ocean as both a wound and a bridge — carrying not just the ghosts of the past, but the echoes of resistance, faith, and rebirth."


In 1996, Farrakhan’s meeting with President Nelson Mandela symbolized a convergence of two spiritual lineages of liberation: one born in Montgomery and Harlem, the other in Soweto and Robben Island. Mandela’s vision of non-racism, non-sexism, and religious tolerance reflected a new dawn; Farrakhan’s fire reminded the world that freedom without truth remains incomplete.


Two years later, in 1998, when he returned to defend Winnie Madikizela-Mandela — calling her “the mother of a great struggle” — he affirmed the feminine divine within resistance, the matriarchal strength that sustains revolutions across oceans.


Through this lens, MAKTUB becomes both testimony and prophecy.
